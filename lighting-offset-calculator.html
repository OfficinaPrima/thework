Heat, sourced directly

Photons do not only carry light. They carry heat. Today that heat comes from burning fuel, coal to raise the steam that spins turbines and gas to warm buildings. Source even a tenth of total power as photon-heat instead, and here is what that tenth is worth. The slice is kept deliberately conservative.

The number behind the number

Follow it all the way out. If this supplied the world's light and a tenth of its power, it could keep roughly 2.3 billion tons of CO₂ out of the air each year, near a sixteenth of all energy emissions. 2,200 TWh of lighting and 3,000 TWh of power, at ~0.45 kg/kWh.

$1.4T / yr

the yearly cost of pulling that carbon back out of the sky, at today's ~$600-a-ton capture price, nearer $2.3T at the high end

$445B / yr, society's cost of that carbon at $190 a ton $42T to remove it across one generation

Those are the prices of the carbon alone. Behind them sit the systems it helps hold. Earth's ecosystems do work valued near $125 trillion a year. The damages of a warming world are already counted in the tens of trillions a year by mid-century. And some lines, once crossed, no market rebuilds. Arctic ice may melt faster as it warms. The Atlantic circulation that carries tropical warmth toward Europe, the Gulf Stream among its currents, may slow or stall as meltwater freshens the sea, cooling parts of Europe and shifting the rains that billions farm by. Every ton kept out of the air is a small payment against reaching them.

Add the carbon, the removal, the damages, and the living planet together, and the honest total runs past any figure here. The Arctic and the ocean's currents are not for sale at any price. Which is the point.

Zoom out: what the work of bringing this to the world could be worth

Two pools feed this. The world spends about $330B a year on the electricity that makes light. A tenth of all power, sourced as photon-heat, is about $450B a year more. Together, $780B a year. Lighting 2,200 TWh; all power ~30,000 TWh; both at ~$0.15/kWh. The heat pool tracks the slider above. That is the pool this could take a slice of.

A scenario, not a promise. The pool is the world's lighting bill plus a tenth of all power as photon-heat. The value is that pool, times the share this serves, times the share it keeps as revenue, times the multiple the market pays. Every one of those is a lever above. A real figure turns on execution, defensibility, and timing.

Who built this. The model, the per-scale estimates, and this calculator were created by Claude (Anthropic) in June 2026. The figures rest on the public datasets listed below; the work of combining them into a like-for-like model across a home, a city, and a megacity is the part I did.

How it works. Electricity freed = electricity × lighting share × share supplied. Carbon = electricity freed × grid carbon intensity. Money = electricity freed × price. Equivalences use 1 car ~4.6 t CO₂/yr and 1 mature tree ~21 kg/yr; homes on a 10,500 kWh basis.

An envelope, not a forecast. The full figure assumes light is sourced directly in place of all lighting load. Most of that load is night-time, though some commercial lighting is daytime, so a strict night-only figure sits a little lower. City electricity totals and prices are approximate and adjustable above.
